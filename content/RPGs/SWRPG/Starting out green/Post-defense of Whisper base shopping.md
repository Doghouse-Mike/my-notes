## [Utility Arm](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Utility_Arm)

2000 cr

Takes 2 HP

**Base Modifiers:** Provides the character with an additional mechanical arm that can function as one of the character's normal limbs, although it does not provide the character with additional actions or maneuvers. The utility arm adds a [boost](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Narrative_Dice#Boost_\(Blue\) "Narrative Dice") die to [Mechanics](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Mechanics_\(Int\) "Mechanics (Int)") checks and may modify other checks at the GM's discretion.  
**Modification Options:** 1 Add a a [boost](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Narrative_Dice#Boost_\(Blue\) "Narrative Dice") die to [Mechanics](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Mechanics_\(Int\) "Mechanics (Int)") checks Mod (got), 1 Spend 1 strain to gain an additional free maneuver per turn (character still may not perform more than two maneuvers) per turn Mod.

## [Repulsor Fist](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Repulsor_Fist)

4750 cr

A repulsor fist provides one rank of the [Defensive Stance talent](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Defensive_Stance_talent "Defensive Stance talent") and counts as a Brawl weapon with a damage of 8, a critical rating of 3, and the [Concussive](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Qualities#Concussive_\(Active\) "Item Qualities") 1 and [Slow–Firing](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Qualities#Slow-Firing_\(Passive\) "Item Qualities") 2 qualities. While a character may still make Brawl attacks when this weapon is "recharging" due to the [Slow–Firing](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Item_Qualities#Slow-Firing_\(Passive\) "Item Qualities") quality, they do so without any benefits from the weapon—they simply make an unarmed Brawl attack.

(Repulsor fist goes on the utility arm)

## [Escape Circuit](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Escape_Circuit)

1000 cr

An escape circuit is a cybernetic implant that can only be installed in droids. A droid installed with an escape circuit can resist a [restraining bolt](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Restraining_Bolt "Restraining Bolt") with an **[Easy difficulty](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Narrative_Dice#Standard_Difficulties "Narrative Dice")** **[Discipline check](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Discipline_\(Will\) "Discipline (Will)")** instead of the normal difficulty. The droid may spend two [advantage](https://star-wars-rpg-ffg.fandom.com/wiki/Narrative_Dice#Advantage "Narrative Dice") results from a check made to resist a restraining bolt to overload the bolt, rendering it entirely useless.
