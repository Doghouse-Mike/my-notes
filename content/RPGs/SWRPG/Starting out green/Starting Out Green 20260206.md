### So We're off to the City

Before we leave, the prisoners are taken away.

Zarlo's fidgeting with his ring again as they're loaded up.

Nasty hobbitses.

Land in one piece. Repairs are underway at the spaceport. None of the staff are in uniform this time around.

They do try to shake us down again. We attempt to convince them that due to our valuable contributions to the city they should let us off, it was not an overwhelming success.

Gamorreans walk out to back him up. 100cr to park. Zarlo forks over 150 but makes it clear that we _haven't been here_. He just wants to make sure everyone gets fed.

The guards are 100% Black Sun. One has a mole on his left jowl, the other looks bored. They're both packing pikes and blasters. We clock a few hoses laying around the place that would start venting pretty good if damaged. The company patching up the place seems local "Iziz WeFix It". Otherwise kinda generic building supplies, nothing notable apart from the barrel on legs as a cement mixer substitute. Wurnco duracrete mixers/suppliers spotted.

The city's in pretty decent shape as we make our way through. Some pickpockets around. No Imperial presence. A lot of construction around. We see a Black Sun person shake down the pickpocket, making them return their gains.

Some of Mr Saw's crew around too. Bit of a stalemate, but the calm is tense. For now.

Get the impression Black Sun are definitely controlling the criminal elements, but we make it to the library without being accosted.

Lots of (paper) books, but also space books, data chips etc. The librarian points us at the local archaeology group/books.

A card falls out of the first book Rimer picks up, belonging to a professor at the local university. The actual book isn't of much use though. Describes the Sith as an ancient religion, dark side of the force etc.

> University professor - Vinson Nalle

Zarlo's asking whether he did the right thing with "the prisoner". Rimer's trying to reassure him. Figures out/fesses up that it's his sister that he's talking about. The last Imperial they met got shot straight up, so that's progress! Zarlo's reminiscing. There's been lots of shooting. Lots of stabbing. Up close n personal stylee. Heost his group a while ago, and almost got captured himself, so prefers keeping his distance now. This situation has brought it all back face to face.

Rimer's reassuring him on the benefit and integral part he's played with us. Zarlo's got the pain and destruction going on. We only do this because we're insane. If he's ever harsh to us, it's because he's trying to fix what's been broken. The nastier stuff comes out when he's _not asking that question_.

Rimer makes the comparison to Buzzsaw's crew, and the losses they've faced, and that we're better than that.

Zarlo _shares his dreams with ghosts_. He's glad we're here though.

Stupid books turning us introspective!

We think of heading to the uni. But the dusk has closed in, so put that off for the next day, and hit the streets, see what info we can pick up. Maybe some bars etc.

Hubbub is that Black Sun are kinda owning the rebuilding. Taking kickbacks, sabotaging anyone not willing to pay.

Buzzsaw's crew have taken over the old Imperial Garrison, maintaining a presence around the barracks and shield generator, but Black Sun are kinda owning most of the city. Word on the street is that it was actually them that caused the liberation. This will not stand.

Blag our way in to the bar for free based on the "everyone gets to keep their arms" discount. That and our help in liberating the city.

KD's fully collar up, hat on.

Local business owners are disgruntled, but the general populace seem happy enough. Buzzsaw's guys are not great at the whole "hearts and minds" bit.

Some cross-referencing with the insider's guide brings up a local cafe that's born a bit of the brunt. Peg's is still open and is our next stop.

She's being slightly evasive, but say everything's very "well protected". Everyone on this strip is feeling the pinch. Commercial district is getting it too. She's glad the building is still standing at least. Not everyone has been so "lucky". Rimer works his charms on her, and she confesses that the Black Sun are fully shaking them down. They're taking a lot of the credit for the liberation too, but she knows it was the Rebellion.

There is a formal union/business owners organisation. But they'll need more backup/actual protection to take a stand. Black Sun has been around for decades, so had fingers everywhere even before all this.

Mr Saw's crew are mostly seen as another group of thugs. Not the face we necessarily want in town. Sticking to their area, rather than pushing out into the city proper.

Peg runs off a few names of businesses that have been burned down. They'd speak out if they weren't so terrified.

Leaning heavily into needing more Alliance support.
