    Is a seller using our shipping labels, or their own? (Only PostNL labels can be ours)

    The first place you’re going to want to check for a success refund is in Admin.

**Duty Manager processes**

- Changing the status to Complete

    Log in to the Merchant portal using the email address from their Admin Portal profile (it’ll be the main asterisked email address).

    Select ‘**Ja**’ in the **Levering** section. Then select ‘**Verstuur met eigen vervoerder**’ (the blue button)

    ![[image 22.png]]

    You’ll get a pop-up with the order details.

    In the drop-down select any courier (Post NL makes sense).

    Unless you know the delivery tracking details, pop Onbekend (Unknown) in Barcode.

    Select Levering as ‘**Ja**’ and hit **Ok**.

    The status will have updated to Complete.

    ![[image 23.png]]

![[Screenshot_2025-07-17_at_13.32.59.png]]

- Changing the status to Cancelled

    > [!important] Remember: this process won’t generate a refund, you’ll need to make sure it goes through Buckaroo

    Log in to the Merchant portal using the email address from their Admin Portal profile (it’ll be the main asterisked email address).

    Select ‘**Nee**’ in the **Levering** section.

    The amount to be sent will remain a 0 if it’s the whole line. If they’ve ordered 2 and 1 needs to be cancelled, select 1 to highlight that 1 will be sent.

    Select ‘**Niet op voorraad’** for Out of Stock.

    Hit Levering doorgeven to submit the cancellation (the green button).

    ![[image 24.png]]

- Adjusting the invoice after issuing a refund (Duty Manager only)

    > [!important] This process is only relevant for those who have access to issue refunds in Buckaroo

    Once the refund has been issued in Buckaroo, the seller’s invoice needs to be adjusted.

    When you’re looking at the sub order, click the seller’s name in **Winkeller** section. From here, select **Order regels**.

    You’ll be presented with all of the recent adjustments. The adjustment form might be at the bottom, scroll all the way down.

    Select **Correctie verrekeningsfactuuur** and enter your reason. You may just want to use ***Correctie retour accepted na uitbetaling**.* Ensure you include the sub order number.

    Click **Toevoegen** to update the invoice

# Seller-specific Considerations and FAQs

> [!important] **What order number?**
> 
> TBD is a store on the V&D website and this can be really confusing.
> 
> When searching for an order in Buckaroo, you need to use the order number linked to the website the order was placed on.
> 
> **How do you know that?** Open the order in both the V&D Admin portal and the TBD Admin portal. Which ever order has the **customer’s** email address (not a TBD or V&D email) is the main order.
> 
> **Hint 2:** if the customer name is Vroom Dreesman (VD), you’re not looking at the correct order to locate it in Buckaroo

> [!important] **Van de Belt Modewereld B.V. Orange line orders**
> 
> Some sellers partially cancel an order and you’ll see the line is Orange in Admin. This means it won’t be shipped.
> 
> **There is no need to contact seller**
> 
> Instead, just check Buckaroo to see whether the refund went through. If not, pop it on the PSP with the reason ‘RF not in PSP’

> [!important] **Keep it Sneaker, and late dispatches**
> 
> “We work with a broad network of international partners. As soon as an order is placed, the sneakers are sent to our main warehouse. There, they are first inspected before being shipped to the customer. This usually takes 1-7 days but can take a little longer if the delivery to us is delayed”

> [!important] **Mike’s Just for Men**
> 
> Late dispatches
> 
> - 14/07/2025 any late dispatches can be cancelled if the customer doesn’t want them. Seller unlikely to ship
> 
> Returns chases
> 
> - If the return shows as delivered over 7 days ago, add to the PSP and **adjust the seller’s invoice**

> [!important] **Generating returns labels on Pending orders**
> 
> **Don’t do it!** Labels generated on pending orders default to TBD’s Head Office.
> 
> Instead, ask CSHelp to update the status of the order for you.
> 
> Once done, the customer can generate the label as normal

> [!important] **Why didn’t the customer receive a full refund?**
> 
> Refunds are charged at 5.75 by order, regardless of how many sellers are included in the return.
> 
> The labels are free to download, however, the 5.75 fee is deducted from the refund of the first item to be refunded.
