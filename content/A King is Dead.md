# A King is Dead
<p>It’s strange how spending <em>years</em> listening to someone’s songs seems like you’re getting to know them. Without ever having talked to Torr, I can still kinda tell that he was one of the good guys. I guess he talked to me, without ever knowing it. Sadly, it’d <a href="http://www.verbicidemagazine.com/2013/06/09/torr-skoog-kings-of-nuthin-dead/">appear he never will</a>. Torr Skoog has left the building.<br/>

![[4AC3E56A-66ED-4FFA-83B6-33967F4B2C1F 1 1.jpeg]]

As frontman of the flat out <em>amazing</em> <a href="http://www.sailorsgraverecords.com/artist.php?id=thekingsofnuthin">Kings of Nuthin</a> he said things that came from a totally different place from where I’m at, but that 99% of the time I could totally identify with. <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vWiF8pWEQKw">Time passing us by</a> (and my lack of doing anything of much consequence with it), <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WuKlmxPWuB0">the “joy” of school</a>, <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5sUj9JZCKtc">not getting on with the lady</a>, <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nbDyx7SlKcQ">crappy jobs</a>, <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iMEnbqmEaeU">being</a> in <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EdIxD4TOD3w">bands</a>, <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N1rhgXsU2yw">and</a> so <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dqkaTmOm2Dw">much</a> <a href="http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zkE2hkNtWF8">more</a>. From the moment I heard these guys I’ve wanted a brass section. They come out looking like a classy swing band or something and then just annihilate you with punk rock. I think the difficulty for them to really fit into any sort of genre hampered them reaching a wider audience, but the lucky ones of us that knew them just lost someone important.<br/>
Just so gutted, thoughts and condolences to the man’s friends, family, bandmates, etc. It’s never good when someone has died, we never get enough time with the ones that count.<br/>
Raise a glass.<br/>
